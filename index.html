Jakob Molinder — Economic Historian

I am an economic historian at Uppsala and Lund University and Director of the Uppsala History of Inequality and Labor Lab (UHILL). My research examines the long-run history of labor markets, wages, income inequality, and the geography of economic development — mostly in Sweden, from the nineteenth century to the present.

I combine newly digitized, micro-level historical data with modern econometric methods to ask how industrialization, technological change, and policy shaped who prospered and who fell behind. I received my PhD from Uppsala University in 2017 and have since been a visiting scholar at the Cambridge Group for the History of Population and Social Structure (CAMPOP), the Swedish Institute for Social Research (SOFI), and University Carlos III in Madrid.

Research interests: Labor markets · Wages · Income inequality · Economic geography · Social mobility · Historical micro-data

What I’m working on

The project closest to me right now is Cities and Socio-Economic Segregation over the Long Run, 1880–2017. Together with a small team I’m piecing together more than a century of geocoded, individual-level records to see who lived where in Swedish cities — and how the map of rich and poor was redrawn as the country industrialized, urbanized, and built the welfare state.

UHILL

I direct the Uppsala History of Inequality and Labor Lab (UHILL) at the Department of Economic History, Uppsala University. The lab studies economic inequality and labor markets from the industrial era to the present, building large historical micro-datasets and applying modern econometric methods to understand the forces that shape economic disparities. We also supervise student work on related themes.
