Research

My work asks how industrialization, technological change, and policy shaped labor markets, wages, and the distribution of income in Sweden over the long run. Much of it is carried out within the UHILL Lab and rests on newly digitized, individual-level historical data.

Current projects

  • Cities and Socio-Economic Segregation over the Long Run, 1880–2017
    Principal investigator · Swedish Research Council (Vetenskapsrådet), 2022–.
    Tracing residential sorting and socio-economic segregation in Swedish cities across more than a century, using geocoded individual-level data to see where different social groups lived and how far apart.
  • The Swedish Transition to Equality: Income Inequality with New Micro Data, 1862–1970
    With Erik Bengtsson & Svante Prado · Jan Wallander & Tom Hedelius Foundation and the Swedish Research Council.
    New individual-level income data to chart how Sweden went from a highly unequal society to one of the world’s most equal — and to identify what actually drove the levelling.
  • Everyone Worked: Work Patterns and Household Labour Allocation in Early Modern Sweden
    With Kathryn Gary & Christopher Pihl · Jan Wallander & Tom Hedelius Foundation.
    Reconstructing how work was organized within households in early modern Sweden, with particular attention to the extent and value of women’s work.
  • Intergenerational Mobility in Sweden Before and After the Welfare State
    With Thor Berger, Björn Eriksson & Per Engzell · Jan Wallander & Tom Hedelius Foundation.
    Linking historical and modern records to measure social mobility across generations and to ask how the rise of the welfare state changed the transmission of advantage.
  • Regulations and the Labour Market in Nineteenth-Century Sweden
    Principal investigator · Jan Wallander & Tom Hedelius Foundation.
    How labour-market regulation shaped wages, mobility, and conflict during Sweden’s industrialization.
